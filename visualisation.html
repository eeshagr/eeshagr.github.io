Visualisation

We chose to work with Billboard Hot 100 Data due to the wide representation that it portrays.

Methodology - Visualisations

Since we aimed to present our data in an interactive and accessible manner, while having limited coding experience, we opted to code in Python and using Plotly packages. Plotly allowed us to have to visualise the values in graphs when hovering over it but also allowed the user to engage with the graph by focusing on certain aspects such as time periods.

Line graph: Number of artist namesâ€™ appearances on the top chart from 1980 to 2019

  • Why did we choose this visualisation: In our project, we used line graphs mainly in order to express trends that arose from top charts, Billboard Hot 100 Data, throughout time. The purpose of this plot is to determine whether the diversity of artists in top charts has fluctuated since the prevail of streaming services.
  • How did we do it: In the Billboard Hot 100 data, we have the name of the artists that have appeared on the chart for each week from 1980 to 2019. Therefore, the number of times an artist's name appears translates to how much he was listened to, how popular he was with the general public.
