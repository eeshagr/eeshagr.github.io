Streaming services and popular music

A data analysis project for UCL's Quantitative Methods 2

research question

How have streaming services modified popular music’s consumption and production?

Aims

The main aim of our investigation is to evaluate the impact of streaming services on popular music’s consumption and production. What is music consumption? We define music consumption as listening habits of consumers that involve the diversity of artists who appear on charts, the diversity in the genres and the time songs spend on charts. What is music production? We define music production as the characteristics of the songs such as duration, key, acousticness, danceability, energy, instrumentalness, liveness, loudness, speechiness, valence, tempo.

Hypothesis

The kind of music we listen to today is influenced by the way we consume music - in our case, consumption through music streaming services transforms popular music. On music consumption after the appearance of music streaming services:
  • New artists break out easier
  • Songs spend less time on the charts
  • Ranking of songs have a different pattern
  • Diversity in artists and genres increases
    •  On music production after the appearance of music streaming services:
  • Songs that are on the top charts become increasingly shorter
  • Songs that are on the top charts are less complex (e.g more repetition)
  • Greater focus on characteristics such as tempo and liveliness
